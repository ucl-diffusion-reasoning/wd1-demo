TL;DR: We introduce \(\mathtt{wd1}\), a simple yet effective and computationally efficient weighted policy optimization method for reasoning in diffusion-based large language models (dLLMs).

Challenges

A key challenge in applying reinforcement learning (RL) to dLLMs is the intractability of their likelihood functions. Existing RL methods for dLLMs rely heavily on the likelihood approximation \( \pi \approx \phi^{\pi} \) [1-3]:

Likelihood approximation introduces notable computational overhead and can induce large bias in the policy ratio.



Method

We propose \(\mathtt{wd1}\), with objective derived from the reverse-KL-constrained policy optimization objective, which dispenses with explicit policy ratios and relies on a single likelihood \( \pi_{\theta} \) :

The aim of \(\mathtt{wd1}\) is simple: increasing the probability of completions with higher \(\hat{A}_i\) values and decreasing it for lower ones, reinforcing beneficial outcomes and discouraging detrimental ones.




Experiments

\( \mathtt{wd1} \) without supervised fine-tuning, consistently outperforms our reproduced implementation of \( \mathtt{d1} \). Notably, \( \mathtt{wd1} \) surpasses \( \mathtt{d1} \) by 8% in test accuracy on the Sudoku, and achieves up to a 25% improvement on Countdown with maximum length 256. Relative to the base LLaDA model, the performance gain reaches 38%.
